## 1. Heroes / Roles / Balance Controversies

Honor of Kings (HoK / 王者荣耀) uses five roles: 射手 (ADC/marksman), 法师 (mage), 坦克 (tank), 战士 (fighter), 辅助 (support) — plus 打野 (jungler). The jungler role is the most contentious: "野区税务稽查员 (jungle tax auditor)" is the community nickname for junglers who take farm from other lanes without productive ganking, which feeds the `jungle-auditor` archetype.

Top-tier heroes as of 2025: Miyamoto Musashi (fighter), Huang Zhong (ADC long-range pick), and several mage supports. Zhong Kui remains the perpetual "if you know, you know" pick for veteran players [no source, community lore].

Skin system is central to HoK identity: limited-edition skins for Chinese New Year and collaborations (e.g., IP crossovers with Chinese entertainment) generate massive social media traffic. The `skin-collector` archetype's entire identity is organized around this. Sensor Tower confirmed HoK as top global mobile revenue in April 2026. Source: [Sensor Tower April 2026](https://sensortower.com/blog/top-10-worldwide-mobile-games-by-revenue-and-downloads-in-april-2026).

## 3. Esports / Competitive Culture

KPL (King Pro League) is the premier HoK competition. The 2025 Grand Finals at Beijing National Stadium set the Guinness record for largest live esports attendance: 62,196 people. Source: [Guinness World Records 2025](https://www.guinnessworldrecords.com/news/commercial/2025/11/62000-honor-of-kings-fans-make-history-as-largest-ever-audience-for-an-esports-match).

Level Infinite restructured HoK's global league in 2026 — the KML (King Master League) now covers Indonesia, Philippines, Malaysia, Brazil, Korea, Japan, South Asia, and MENA. Source: [Esports Insider Dec 2025](https://esportsinsider.com/2025/12/honor-of-kings-creates-new-global-esports-league-2026). This means HoK now has multiple regional pro scenes generating local vocabulary.

Team rivalries and player celebrities generate Bilibili clip compilations and Weibo trending topics. The `teamfight-festival` archetype peaks during KPL team fights — the 10-person clashes that make even non-players stop scrolling.

## 4. Streamer / Content-Creator Vocabulary

Bilibili and Douyin dominate zh HoK content. Streamer phrases: "打野你在干嘛 (jungler what are you doing)" is the most-typed phrase in Chinese MOBA history [no source, community lore]. "你们有大吗 (does anyone have ult)" before a teamfight is the equivalent of the support asking for help [no source, community lore].

"三万 gank (30,000 gank)" refers to a jungler who only ganks when a lane already has a 30,000-gold lead — useless help at the wrong moment [no source, community lore]. This is `jungle-auditor` culture documented.

Five-stack Douyin compilations — groups of friends playing together, mixing voice chat chaos with in-game action — are a primary growth format. `family-host` is the player who initiated the five-stack, manages everyone's schedule, and calms the fight about who's playing which role [no source, community lore].

## 5. Server / Rank Culture

Rank system: Bronze → Silver → Gold → Platinum → Diamond → Master → Grandmaster → Honor King (王者段位). "王者" literally means "king/monarch" — reaching the top rank shares a name with the game. This creates a particular pride topology [no source, community lore].

"钻石地狱 (Diamond hell)" is the rank where the game becomes genuinely competitive but matchmaking is unstable. The `solo-curse` archetype spends most of their career here [no source, community lore].

Star rating within ranks adds a granular status layer. Players share screenshots of their star progress the same way CS2 players share Premier Rating updates [no source, community lore].

## 8. Connections to Archetypes

`jungle-auditor` enforces "野区税" collection — they know who took their farm, and they will mention it. `family-host` built the five-stack WeChat group and manages the emotional energy of four very different people trying to win. `defeat-grief-counselor` always votes No on the surrender — someone has to, and they genuinely believe a comeback is possible every time. `teamfight-festival` queues only for the 10-person clashes and considers backing out of a fight a personal failure. `lane-pressure-artist` pings before the tower dive to let people know it's intentional, not an error. `skin-collector` took a screenshot when their limited-skin hero got a kill; winning without showing the skin counts as a partial loss. `solo-curse` types "再来一把" at 2 AM with full sincerity. `bm-ping-mayor` has a rotation of question-mark pings mapped to specific teammate actions — each one precise, each one deniable as an accident.
